The Bangladesh Liberation War (1971) was caused by political exclusion, economic disparity, cultural repression and military brutality by West Pakistan. 

In the light of Lord Acton’s theory—“Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely”
—the war exemplifies how unchecked authority in West Pakistan led to corruption, oppression and ultimately the violent struggle for independence.

🔑 Key Causes of the Liberation War

• 	Political Exclusion
• 	Economic Exploitation
• 	Cultural Repression
• 	Military Brutality

📖 Lord Acton’s Theory of Power

* Core Idea: “Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely.”

* Implication: Concentrated authority inevitably leads to abuse, moral decay, and disregard for justice.

## Application to Bangladesh : 

• 	West Pakistan’s rulers monopolized political, economic, and military power.

• 	This unchecked authority led to corruption, exploitation, and systemic repression.

• 	The denial of democratic rights after the 1970 election victory of Awami League illustrates Acton’s warning: rulers corrupted by absolute power refused to transfer authority peacefully.

~#~ Cause of War and Acton's Theory Connection : 

1. Political exclusion

-- Concentration of power corrupts democracy

Ex : Awami League’s majority ignored in 1970 elections

2. Economic disparity

-- Absolute authority exploits resources

Ex : East Pakistan’s jute revenue drained to West Pakistan

3. Cultural repression

-- Power suppresses liberty and identity

Ex : Urdu imposition, banning of Tagore’s works

4. Military brutality

-- Absolute power breeds tyranny

Ex : Operation Searchlight genocide

5. Moral Corruption

-- Leaders prioritized control over justice

📌 Conclusion
The Bangladesh Liberation War shows how absolute power leads to corruption and oppression. 
West Pakistan’s rulers abused authority, denied freedom and committed genocide. 
This sparked resistance and Bangladesh’s independence became both a fight against tyranny and proof of Lord Acton’s warning that unchecked power always corrupts.
