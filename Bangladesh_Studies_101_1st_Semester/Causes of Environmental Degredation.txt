Environmental degradation means the environment is being damaged when air, water and soil are polluted or depleted, forests and ecosystems are destroyed and plants and animals disappear, making the earth less healthy and less able to support life.

The United Nations International Strategy for Disaster Reduction defines environmental degradation as 
“the reduction of the capacity of the environment to meet social and ecological objectives and needs”.

** Causes

~~ I. Social Factors

1. Huge Population

-- 👉 Super simple: More people → more resource use → more waste → nature gets damaged.

2. Poverty

-- 👉 Super simple: Poverty hurts nature, and hurt nature makes poverty deeper.


3. Urbanization

-- 👉 Super simple: No jobs in villages → people move to towns → cities grow badly → slums expand.

~~ II. Economic Factors

1. Market Failure

-- 👉 Super simple: Profitable activities cause greenhouse gases, but others pay the price, so emissions keep rising.

2. Transport Activities

-- 👉 Super simple: Transport makes air dirty, noisy, and unsafe, causing health problems in urban Bangladesh.

3. Economic Development

-- 👉 Super simple: Industries grow the economy but pollute nature and damage ecosystems.

~~ III. Political and Administrative Factors

1. Lack of growing environmental awareness
2. Lack of growing impact of environmental law
3. Influential people does not obey law
4. Lack of strong political will

~~IV. Environmental Factors

1. Habitat Fragmentation

-- 👉 Super simple: Building roads or towns splits nature into pieces, hurting plants, animals and their homes.
