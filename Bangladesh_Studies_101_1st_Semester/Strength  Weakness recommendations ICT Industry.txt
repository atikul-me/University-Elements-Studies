Strengths....

1. Young, energetic, educated workforce with workable English skill.

2. Over 15,000 IT engineers are working in over 400 software & IT service companies, around 150 of these are specialized in serving overseas clients.

3. Salary of programmers in Bangladesh is 50% of that in India, 40% of Philippines and 70% of the Vietnam.

4.Bangladeshi services are better priced than other offshore destinations. 

5. Hardware platforms necessary for IT development are available.

6. The demand for computers and peripherals is booming.

7. Rent for office space in Dhaka is below 20% of that in Delhi and 40% of Manila.

8. Charges for Internet bandwidth in Bangladesh is currently the lowest on South Asia (50% of that in India).


Weakness...

1. Bangladeshi market remains infrastructure and power shortages.

2. Low per capita income compared to the cost of PC & internet.

3. Absence of local contents on the internet.

4. Lack of skilled ICT teachers & trainers.

5. Inadequate internet speed compared to international standard.

6. Poor international visibility and lack of brand name.

7. Small ICT companies seem to lack focus to take up large long-term projects.

8. There is a gap between the industry needs and the IT graduates.

9. Lack of soft skills in Bangladesh. If you have 100 candidates for the job, probably just 2 have the right soft skills.

10. Political Instability hampers the development of ICT Industry. 

~#~  recommend to improve the quality of ICT/Software İndustry in Bangladesh

To improve the quality of Bangladesh’s ICT and software industry, the country must focus on talent upskilling, infrastructure modernization, global competitiveness and stronger policy support. 
Aligning with “Smart Bangladesh 2041,” investment in AI, cloud, cybersecurity and startup ecosystems will be critical.

📌 Key Recommendations

1. Human Capital Development

• 	Upskilling Programs: Expand coding bootcamps, AI/ML training, and cybersecurity certifications.

• 	University-Industry Collaboration: Align curricula with industry needs (cloud, blockchain, DevOps).

• 	Freelancer Empowerment: Bangladesh has over 750,000 ICT professionals, many freelancers; structured training and global marketplace access can boost exports.

2. Infrastructure & Ecosystem

• 	Hi-Tech Parks Expansion: Current ICT parks should be scaled with better internet, power reliability, and incubation facilities.

• 	Cloud & Data Centers: Encourage local cloud providers to reduce reliance on foreign services.

• 	Cybersecurity Frameworks: Strengthen national cybersecurity laws and enterprise-level protections.

3. Policy & Governance

• 	Ease of Doing Business: Simplify regulations, reduce tax burdens, and streamline export processes.

• 	Smart Bangladesh 2041 Vision: Implement universal digital ID, blended learning, and citizen upskilling programs.

• 	Stable Political Climate: Investor confidence depends on predictable governance and reduced unrest.

4. Global Competitiveness

• 	Focus on Emerging Tech: AI, blockchain, cloud computing, and cybersecurity are global growth areas.

• 	Export Diversification: Move beyond outsourcing to product innovation (e.g., SaaS platforms).

• 	Branding “Made in Bangladesh ICT”: Promote Bangladesh as a reliable software hub internationally.

5. Startup & Innovation Support

• 	Funding Access: Expand venture capital and government seed funds.

• 	Collaboration Platforms: Build digital collaboration hubs for startups and SMEs.

• 	Global Partnerships: Encourage joint ventures with Indian, Chinese, and Western tech firms.

** invest in skills, infrastructure, and innovation while ensuring political stability and cybersecurity resilience.
